==================================================================================================================
HOW THE HEURISTICS WERE RUN
==================================================================================================================
None of the [HEURISTICS] parameters are hard physical constraints, they are operating points. The standard
way to pick an operating point in MD is to measure it, so heuristics.py runs one calibration sweep per
parameter, using only standard, textbook procedures:

    S1  Timestep convergence      NVE energy drift vs dt; velocity-Verlet must show drift ~ dt^2
                                  (Allen & Tildesley; Frenkel & Smit - the canonical dt test)
    S2  Equilibration time        settling time of T(t) and U(t) from the hot start, 8 seeds
    S3  Autocorrelation analysis  velocity-ACF and potential-energy-ACF, integrated correlation time
                                  (Sokal windowing), effective independent sample count
    S4  Convergence (knee) study  g(r) vs number of frames and number of bins, like a mesh-refinement study
    S5  Noise floors              seed-replica std across 10 independent seeds, cross-checked with
                                  Flyvbjerg-Petersen block averaging (J. Chem. Phys. 91, 461 (1989))
    S6  Threshold separation      distribution of the crystal and liquid g(r) peaks over 10 seeds
    S7  Sensitivity check         per-bin noise vs Poisson prediction; verdict vs number of skipped bins
    S8  Invariance check          equilibrium T and structure vs Langevin friction (must be flat)

Every sweep replicates the EXACT test.py protocol (same lattice start, same thermostat, same sampling
rules) and only varies the one variable under study. Heuristic Outputs can be seen in the heuristics_out/
directory. Reproduce them bhy using python3 heuristics.py --all      (seeds are fixed, results are
deterministic).

Baseline state points, as in test.py: N = 256, kappa = 1.0, dt = 0.01, Gamma_crystal = 400 (T = 0.0025),
Gamma_liquid = 2 (T = 0.5), friction = 1.0, steps = 6000.

1. total_force:
    By Newton's third law every particle produces an equal and opposite force on each other so
    the net force on the system i.e all particle being studied together, all equal and opposite
    force pairs cancel out and must return total_force = 0

    However computers ain't that precise so for the sake of their precision I have set
    total_force < 1e-10

    Why specifically 1e-10?
        Summing N numbers in float64, the rounding error is bounded by roughly
                    (N−1) · ε_machine · Σ|Fₖ|,
        where ε_machine ≈ 2.2e-16. For N=256, and the per-particle force magnitudes are O(1),
        so Σ|Fₖ| ~ O(N) ~ few hundred.

        That gives a worst-case residual ≈ 256 × 2.2×1e-16 × few·1e2 ≈ 1e-11. So the theoretical
        roundoff ceiling is ~1e-11 but for the sake of safety, I chose 1e-10, it does not cause
        passing of faulty cases.

2. energy_drift < 1e-2 (and dt = 0.01)                                                   [S1: s1_dt.json]

    Method: from one equilibrated state per case, integrate with the thermostat OFF (pure velocity-
    Verlet) for a FIXED physical time t = 20 at each dt, and measure the relative drift
    (E_max - E_min)/|E_mean|. Velocity-Verlet is a 2nd-order integrator, so on a log-log plot the
    drift must fall on a slope-2 line; that both validates the forces AND places the threshold.

    Results:
        dt        crystal drift   liquid drift
        0.002     7.40e-10        3.03e-06
        0.005     4.62e-09        1.89e-05
        0.01      1.85e-08        7.63e-05      <- working dt
        0.02      7.39e-08        3.13e-04
        0.04      2.96e-07        7.62e-04
        0.08      1.18e-06        3.35e-02      <- liquid breaches 1e-2 here

        log-log slope: crystal 2.000, liquid 2.32 (the liquid fit is pulled up by the dt = 0.08
        point which sits at the stability edge; over dt <= 0.04 the liquid also scales at ~2.0).

    Justification:
        - The measured slope ~2 confirms the integrator/forces are correct, i.e the drift is not a bug.
        - At the working dt = 0.01 the worst-case (liquid) drift is 7.63e-05, a factor of 131
          below the 1e-2 PASS bound (crystal has a factor ~5.4e5). So the check never fails on a
          healthy integrator.

        - The bound is still reachable: at dt = 0.08 the liquid drift is 3.35e-02 > 1e-2, so a
          genuinely too-large timestep (or a broken force) does trip it. 1e-2 therefore separates
          "worked" from "broken" with ~two orders of magnitude of margin on each side.

        - The MARGINAL band (1e-2..1e-1) covers the region where the run is degraded but not yet
          blown up (the liquid enters it just below dt ~ 0.08).

3. friction = 1.0                                                                  [S8: s8_summary.json]

    Method: invariance check

    Results (mean over 3 seeds):
        gamma     T_mean/T_target (crys)   T_mean/T_target (liq)   peak (crys)   peak (liq)
        0.1       4.058  [NOT settled]     1.004                   3.13          1.013
        0.5       1.032                    0.990                   3.41          1.013
        1.0       1.009                    0.989                   3.24          1.014
        2.0       1.009                    0.999                   3.00          1.012
        5.0       1.011                    1.000                   2.50          1.010

    Justification:
        - The extremes fail: 
            At gamma = 0.1 the coupling is too weak to drain the initial excess energy 
            within 6000 steps because the crystal is underdamped
            
            At gamma = 5 the dynamics are overdamped, the crystal cannot vary its initial 
            jitter too much within the run, and its peak sags to 2.50.

        - In the middle of the range (gamma = 0.5 to 2) both the temperature and the structure are
          flat within the seed spread. 

        - gamma = 1.0 sits in the middle of the usable window [0.5, 2], where equilibration is
          fastest and results are friction-independent. (In reduced units this is also the natural
          O(1) choice)

4. |T_mean - T2| < 0.05*T2  (5% thermostat tolerance)                    [S5: s5_noise_floors.json]

    Method: Noise floor. Run the EXACT test.py thermostat check (hot start at T1 = 2.0, thermostat
    to T2 = 0.0025, average T over step > 1000) with 10 independent seeds; the seed-to-seed spread
    of T_mean IS the statistical noise floor. Cross-check the error bar with Flyvbjerg-Petersen
    block averaging on a single run. A tolerance is justified when it sits several times above the
    floor.

    Results:
        per-seed T_mean/T2:   1.0223  1.0046  1.0193  1.0152  1.0356  1.0350  1.0354  1.0070
                              1.0277  1.0236
        std:                 1.13% of T2
        block-averaging plateau (1 run):  1.00% of T2   (independent estimate; agrees, ratio 0.88)
        worst deviation:      3.56%

        TOLERANCE:          0.05 / 0.0113 = 4.4x [The main result]

    Justification:
        - The 5% tolerance is 4.4x the measured noise floor: a healthy thermostat never fails it
          by bad luck (all 10/10 seeds pass), while a thermostat that is actually biased at the
          5% level is >4 sigma away from noise and gets caught.
        - Two INDEPENDENT error estimates (seed replicas and block averaging) agree to within 12%,
          so the floor itself is trustworthy.
        - Honest finding: all 10 seeds land slightly WARM (mean +2.3%). This is consistent with
          the settling-time result S2 below - the step > 1000 cutoff starts averaging while the
          slowest seeds are still cooling (t_eq up to ~1400 steps), so part of the transient leaks
          into the average. The tolerance still clears the worst seed by 1.4x, but this is why the
          tolerance cannot be tightened below ~4% without also raising the cutoff (see FINDINGS).

5. frames = 50                                                            [S4: s4_gr_convergence.json]

    Method: Convergence. Collect 200 decorrelation-spaced frames per case, average g(r) over the first k 
    of them, and measure the RMS relative difference from the full 200-frame average.

    Results (RMS relative difference, bins >= 5):
        k frames    crystal     liquid
        10          5.5%        2.5%
        25          4.6%        1.7%
        50          3.6%        0.94%     <- standard working value
        100         2.2%        0.63%

    Justification:
        - At 50 frames the g(r) average is within 3.6% (crystal) / 0.94% (liquid) of the fully
          converged curve, and the curve is monotonically converging - 50 sits past the knee.
        - 3.6% residual scatter is far below anything the structure check depends on: the
          crystal/liquid peak separation it must resolve is a factor ~3.2 (S6), i.e. ~220%.
        - Also verified from the normalization side: with 50 ideal-gas frames the g_mean tolerance
          is 0.0007 (S5), 70x below its 0.05 tolerance.

6. bins = 80                                                              [S4: s4_gr_convergence.json]

    Method: resolution/statistics trade-off sweep. More bins resolve the peak better (height rises
    toward its true value) but put fewer pair counts in each bin (noise rises). Sweep bins over
    40..240 at fixed frames and measure both.

    Results:
        bins    peak (crystal)   peak (liquid)   median per-bin noise (ideal gas, 50 frames)
        40      2.456            1.006           0.0038
        60      3.138            1.010           0.0047
        80      3.224            1.010           0.0054     <- working value
        120     3.460            1.021           0.0066
        160     3.524            1.017           0.0076
        240     3.589            1.025           0.0093

    Justification:
        - Resolution: at 80 bins the crystal peak (3.22) is resolved to within ~10% of the
          finest-grid value (3.59 at 240 bins), and - the part the verdict cares about - the
          structure check would PASS at every bin count from 60 up. Only at 40 bins does the
          under-resolved crystal peak (2.456) dip below the 2.5 threshold, so 40 is too coarse;
          the ratio criterion (worst case 2.44x vs threshold 1.5x) survives even there.
        - Statistics: at 80 bins the median per-bin noise of the normalization test is 0.0054,
          ~9x below its 0.05 tolerance, so the extra resolution costs nothing that matters.
        - 80 therefore sits in the flat region where BOTH tests are insensitive to the choice:
          anywhere in 60..240 gives the same verdicts.

7. |g_mean - 1| < 0.05  (the g(r) normalization tolerance)                   [S5: s5_noise_floors.json]

    Method: noise floor, same logic as parameter 4. Run the exact test.py normalization check
    (50 uniform-random frames, 80 bins, skip 5) with 10 independent seeds.

    Results:
        per-seed g_mean:    0.9962  0.9957  0.9959  0.9975  0.9963  0.9969  0.9951  0.9961
                            0.9972  0.9966
        seed-to-seed std:               0.0007
        worst deviation from 1:         0.0049
        tolerance / noise floor:        0.05 / 0.0007 = 70x

    Justification:
        - The tolerance is 70x the noise floor: this check effectively never fails by chance.
          What it does catch is a broken normalization - the classic bugs (forgetting the annulus
          area, the frame count, or the density in the denominator) shift g_mean by factors of
          O(2..N), thousands of noise floors, so the wide margin costs no detection power.
        - There is a tiny systematic offset (all seeds ~0.4% below 1, a finite-N effect: the
          histogram counts N(N-1) ordered pairs while the ideal-gas normalization assumes N^2 -
          a deficit of exactly 1/N = 0.4%). It is 12x smaller than the tolerance and does not
          accumulate; noted here for honesty.

8. peak_crystal > 2.5  and  peak_crystal > 1.5 * peak_liquid                  [S6: s6_summary.json]

    Method: threshold separation. Run the EXACT test.py structure protocol (6000 steps, frames from
    the second half every 20 steps, 80 bins) 10 times per case with independent seeds, and look at
    where the two peak distributions actually sit. A threshold is justified when it lies in a wide
    empty gap between them.

    Results (10 seeds per case):
        peak_crystal:  min 3.13   mean 3.27   max 3.56   (std 0.12)
        peak_liquid:   min 1.010  mean 1.015  max 1.025  (std 0.005)
        gap between the distributions: 2.11 (no overlap - the ranges are 2 units apart)
        2.5 threshold:  lowest crystal peak clears it by 1.25x; highest liquid peak is at 0.41x
        ratio check:    worst paired ratio peak_crystal/peak_liquid = 3.08  vs threshold 1.5

    Justification:
        - The absolute threshold 2.5 sits in the middle of a gap that is ~17 crystal-side standard
          deviations wide: over 10 independent runs neither distribution comes near it from either
          side. The check cannot misclassify either state point by statistical fluctuation.
        - The ratio criterion holds with a 2x margin in its worst seed pairing (3.08 vs 1.5).
        - Physical sanity: the liquid at Gamma = 2 is nearly structureless (peak ~1.02), while the
          crystal peak ~3.3 is characteristic of a strongly ordered first shell - the two-sided
          check (absolute AND relative) is what makes the verdict robust to both failure modes
          (no order at all, and "everything looks the same").

9. steps = 6000                                                          [S2: s2_equilibration.json]

    Method: measure the settling time and check the run length leaves enough measurement window
    after it. From the hot start (T1 = 2.0 -> T2 = 0.0025, the harshest transient any test.py run
    faces), 8 seeds:

    Results:
        temperature settling t_eq(T):     1048..1386 steps  (worst 1386)
        potential-energy settling t_eq(U): 3370..4300 steps  (worst 4300)
        (U is the slow mode for all 8/8 seeds, as expected - the velocities cool in ~1/gamma but
         the CONFIGURATION must recrystallize, which is much slower.)

    Justification:
        - 6000 steps covers the worst measured settling (4300) with a 1.4x margin AND still leaves
          a >=1700-step fully-equilibrated window for measurement. Since the harshest transient in
          the ladder settles inside the run, 6000 is sufficient for every check that uses it.
        - The margin is honest but not generous (see FINDINGS: the cutoffs INSIDE the run are the
          things that need moving, not the run length - though 8000-10000 steps would buy comfort).

10. every = 20                                                           [S3: s3_decorrelation.json]

    Method: autocorrelation analysis. Frames carry full statistical weight only if sampled at
    intervals > ~2x the integrated autocorrelation time tau_int. Measured from a 4000-step
    equilibrated production run per case:

    Results:
                                crystal         liquid
        tau_int (velocity ACF)  65 steps        61 steps
        tau_int (PE ACF)        413 steps (*)   51 steps
        every = 20 -> effective independent frames out of the 149 collected:
                                ~3.6 (*)        ~29
        (*) window-limited: the crystal PE-ACF has not decayed to zero within the 600-step
            window, so 413 is a LOWER bound - crystal configurations are correlated over
            essentially the whole run.

    Honest finding: every = 20 does NOT produce statistically independent frames - the liquid
    frames decorrelate in ~100 steps and the crystal configurations essentially never do (a solid
    barely explores configuration space; its frames are inherently similar - that is what being a
    crystal MEANS, not a sampling bug).

    Why the test is still valid as written:
        - Correlated frames do not BIAS a g(r) average - they only reduce the effective sample
          count, i.e. they inflate its variance. For the crystal, frame-averaging is doing thermal
          smoothing, not independent sampling - and that is all the structure check needs.
        - The verdict-level noise this actually produces is measured DIRECTLY by S6: across 10
          fully independent runs the crystal peak scatters by only std = 0.12 (3.7%), against a
          separation gap of 2.11. The correlation between frames within a run is therefore
          irrelevant to the PASS/FAIL outcome.
        - If truly independent frames were ever needed (e.g. for error bars ON g(r) itself),
          the measured taus say: every >= ~100 for the liquid, and for the crystal use
          independent-seed runs instead of longer sampling.

R1. Why average only T_samples with step > 1000?  (test.py L67)          [S2: s2_equilibration.json]

    The cutoff must exceed the measured settling time of the quantity being averaged. S2 measured
    (hot start, 8 seeds): T settles at 1048..1386 steps.

    FINDING - the current value is NOT justified: 1000 is slightly EARLY. Every one of the 8 seeds
    still has a cooling tail beyond step 1000 (worst 1386), and this is visible as the small
    systematic warm bias (+2.3%) in S5's T_mean replicas. The check still passes with margin
    (worst seed 3.56% vs the 5% tolerance) because the leaked transient is small, but the honest,
    measured cutoff is:

        cutoff >= ~1400 (worst measured t_eq(T)), i.e. 2000 with a 1.4x safety factor.

    APPLIED: test.py now uses step > 2000. Cost: none (the averaging window shrinks
    from 5000 to 4000 samples; the noise floor grows by sqrt(5/4) ~ 12%, still 4x below
    tolerance). Benefit: removes the known bias source instead of tolerating it.
    (S5's quoted +2.3% warm bias was measured at the OLD 1000 cutoff and is left as the
    record of WHY 1000 was wrong; re-run S5 if a fresh post-fix noise floor is wanted.)

R2. Why skip exactly the first 5 bins?  (test.py L87)                        [S7: s7_bin_skip.json]

    Method: (a) identify WHICH bins are unreliable - per-bin seed-to-seed scatter across 10 seeds,
    compared with the Poisson expectation 1/sqrt(expected pair counts); (b) sensitivity - re-run
    the verdict for every skip value 0..12 and see if the choice matters.

    Results:
        bin   r-center   expected counts   Poisson noise   measured seed std
        0     0.089      402               5.0%            10.0%
        1     0.266      1206              2.9%            3.7%
        2     0.443      2011              2.2%            3.9%
        3     0.620      2815              1.9%            3.2%
        4     0.798      3619              1.7%            1.7%
        5     0.975      4423              1.5%            1.9%     <- skip boundary
        ...   (noise keeps falling ~ 1/sqrt(r) from here on)

        Verdict sensitivity: worst |g_mean - 1| across seeds is 0.0060 at skip = 0 and 0.0049 at
        skip = 5 - PASS at EVERY skip from 0 to 12, tolerance 0.05.

    Justification:
        - The innermost bins are measurably the unreliable ones (bin 0 scatters at 2x its already-
          worst Poisson floor - annulus area -> 0, counts -> few), which is the reason for
          skipping a small-r region at all.
        - But the VERDICT does not care exactly how many are skipped: it passes identically for
          skip 0..12. So "5" is a convention inside a wide flat plateau, not a tuned value - the
          honest answer to "why not 6?" is that 6 (or 0, or 12) gives the same verdict; 5 skips
          precisely the noisy sub-first-shell region r < ~0.9a.

R3. Why sample structure frames only for step > steps//2, every 20?  (test.py L110)   [S2 + S3]

    Two knobs in one line:

    step > steps//2 (= 3000): the equilibration guard for structure sampling. Justified with a
    caveat: the structure runs start ON a lattice AT the target temperature (not the hot start
    S2 measured), so their transient is far milder than S2's worst case of 4300 steps - the
    crystal run starts essentially IN its equilibrium phase and only anneals the initial jitter,
    and the liquid melts from the superheated lattice within a few hundred steps. The direct
    evidence that 3000 is late enough for THIS protocol is again S6: if frames from 3000..4300
    were meaningfully unequilibrated, the 10-seed peak distributions would be biased and wide -
    measured, they scatter by only 3.7% (crystal) and 0.5% (liquid). For the harshest (hot-start)
    protocol, steps//2 would NOT be enough (S2: t_eq(U) up to 4300) - do not reuse this line for
    hot starts.

    every = 20: see parameter 10 above - not an independence interval (measured tau_int is larger),
    but a thinning convention whose statistical cost is bounded directly by S6 and is irrelevant
    to the verdict. Its practical value: it cuts the memory/CPU of g(r) accumulation by 20x
    relative to keeping every frame, at zero verdict cost.

FINDINGS SUMMARY (what the calibration actually changed my mind about)
==================================================================================================================
Ran: 8 sweeps, 13 shards, ~50 full simulations, 10 seeds where it matters. Verdicts:

    SUPPORTED as-is:   dt = 0.01, drift < 1e-2 (S1) | 5% T tolerance (S5) | frames = 50, bins = 80
                       (S4) | 0.05 g_mean tolerance (S5) | peak thresholds 2.5 and 1.5x (S6) |
                       skip = 5 (S7) | steps = 6000 (S2) | friction = 1.0 within [0.5, 2] (S8)

    CONTRADICTED:
    1. step > 1000 cutoff (test.py L67): measured T settling is 1048..1386 steps - the cutoff
       starts averaging early on every seed and causes the +2.3% warm bias seen across all S5
       replicas. RECOMMENDATION: raise to step > 2000. (Check still passes today; this is bias
       hygiene, not a broken test.)
    2. every = 20 as an "independence" interval (S3): frames 20 steps apart are strongly
       correlated (tau_int: liquid ~51 steps, crystal >= 413 and window-limited). Reframed
       honestly as a thinning convention; test validity is instead established by the 10-seed
       replica spread (S6). NO code change needed - but the justification had to change.

    WOULD FAIL IF CHANGED CARELESSLY (useful to know the cliff edges):
       bins < 60 under-resolves the crystal peak below the 2.5 threshold (S4);
       friction outside [0.5, 2] needs longer runs (gamma = 0.1 never settles, gamma = 5
       cannot anneal the crystal in 6000 steps) (S8);
       dt >= ~0.08 breaks NVE for the liquid (S1).
